Top 10 dishes of Manali

1)Bombay Bhel Puri

Spicy and tangy this street food of Mumbai is easily available in Manali too. Being a concoction of the fried rice, onions, potatoes, chick peas, tangy tamarind sauce and much more, this delicacy is hard to leave once tasted. For the people having this dish for the first time, the taste and the experience both seem incomparably delightful

2)Samosa

The ever favorite Indian snack is a hot favorite in Manali too, the reason being it proves to be the right respite from the chilling weathers at this hill station. Almost any place in Manali can be found equipped with this dish. Precisely this dish is a deep-fried hot snack made of refined flour with a filling of potatoes and other seasonings in it.

3)Himachali Khatta

A delicacy most common in the Himachali food, Khatta is a preparation of pumpkin, dry mango powder and crisp little balls called boondi. It is only this state that the true versions of this food can be found and therefore Manali comes to pride itself in selling this on the food street Manali

4)Sidu

Another local dish that is a must-have in Manali is Sidu. Being the ideal companion for mutton and other non-vegetarian Indian preparations, this dish is a prepared by with the dough of the bread. The refined flour dough for bread is first kept aside to rise and once the yeast has settled down on it, the dough is par-cooked. Then this par cooked bread is steamed. Finally, this dish is prepared and a simple taste of the food leaves you asking for more.

5)Babru

Another of the best street food in Manali is Babru. This is a dish belonging to the family of the age-old Indian snack of kachoris. Quite like the kachoris, this dish too is prepared out of using the soaked black gram paste and then kneading it to create this dish.

6)Trout

One of the best and the most unique preparations of the Manali and Kullu region is trout. The preparations of this fish in this area are done by marinating the raw material and finally slow cooking them for that impeccable effect. Quite good for the health, unlike many street foods, this dish is low in calories too.

7)Patande

Along with being one of the most picturesque states of India, Himachal Pradesh is also home to one of the most exotic dishes in the country. Patande is one such breakfast dish which originates in the Sirmour district of Himachal Pradesh. Also referred to as the pancake of India, patande is prepared by cooking a batter made of wheat flour, sugar, milk, baking powder in spices like cardamom and cinnamon. This easy to make dish requires less time and no elaborate preparations. The minimum spices used in the dish make it ideal for kids as well as adults. You will enjoy making this dish as much as your family members will enjoy eating it. If you are the kind of person who always likes to serve something new and different, this recipe is perfect for you. Prepare this dish at your next kitty party, potluck or buffet and amaze your family and friends with your diverse cooking skills. This dish will definitely make you the star of any event and will leave your guests wanting for more. Prepared under 40 minutes, these patandes are also ideal to pack for lunch and to be enjoyed as a quick in-between work bite. Pair this delectable dish with apple jam, maple syrup or a simple piping hot cup of tea and enjoy its goodness with your loved ones!

8)Bhey ki Sabzi

A preparation using the lotus stems, Bhey is a dish that owes its distinct flavors to the unique texture of the raw materials. This delectable is prepared by thinly slicing the lotus stem, streaming it and then sautéing it in a carried collection of the herbs, spices and gram flour. 10)Gulab Jamun

Gulab jamun (also spelled gulaab jamun) is a milk-solid-based sweet from the Indian subcontinent, and a type of mithai, popular in India, Nepal, Pakistan (where it is known as gulab jamun), the Maldives (where it is known as gulaabujaanu), and Bangladesh (where it is known as gulab jam), as well as Myanmar. It is also common in nations with substantial populations of people with South Asian heritage, such as Mauritius, Fiji, the Malay Peninsula, Great Britain, South Africa, and the Caribbean countries of Trinidad and Tobago, (where it is called rasgulla), Guyana, and Suriname. It is made mainly from milk solids, traditionally from khoya, which is milk reduced to the consistency of a soft dough. Modern recipes call for dried or powdered milk instead of khoya. It is often garnished with dried nuts such as almonds and cashews to enhance flavour.
